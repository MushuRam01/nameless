Our Philosophy

In 1918, mathematician G.H. Hardy visited his colleague Srinivasa Ramanujan in hospital. Hardy mentioned that he had arrived in taxi number 1729, calling it "a rather dull number."

Ramanujan immediately replied, "No, it is a very interesting number; it is the smallest number expressible as the sum of two cubes in two different ways."

This moment perfectly captures our approach to law: where others see the ordinary, we discover the extraordinary. We find the hidden connections, the overlooked opportunities, and the profound solutions that make all the difference.
